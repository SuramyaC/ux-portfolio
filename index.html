About Me

Your Photo

I'm a UX researcher with a background in cognitive science and psychology. Currently finishing my M.Sc. in Cognitive Science at RPTU Kaiserslautern, I specialize in understanding how people process information and interact with complex systems.

My work sits at the intersection of research rigor and design impact — I use methods like eye-tracking, usability testing, and behavioral experiments to uncover insights that directly shape product decisions. I've applied this at KUKA (robotics), DFKI (AI research), and the Max Planck Institute.

I'm most energized when research reveals something unexpected — the kind of insight that reframes a problem and opens up better solutions.

Research Methods

Tools and approaches I bring to every project

👁️

Eye-Tracking

Gaze analysis using EyeLink systems to understand attention, reading behavior, and interface interaction patterns.

🧪

Usability Testing

Moderated and unmoderated sessions using SUS, UEQ, and behavioral data to evaluate product experiences.

🎙️

User Interviews

In-depth qualitative conversations to uncover motivations, mental models, and unmet needs.

🥽

VR Research

Designing immersive experiments in Unity to study cognition, spatial attention, and embodied interaction.

📊

Quantitative Analysis

Statistical modeling (ANOVA, mixed-effects) in R and SPSS to validate findings and measure impact at scale.

🗺️

Mixed-Methods Synthesis

Combining qualitative insights with quantitative evidence to build a complete picture and drive alignment.
